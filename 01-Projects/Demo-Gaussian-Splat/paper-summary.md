# Vision-Only Gaussian Splatting for Collaborative Semantic Occupancy Prediction

## Summary

Self-driving vehicles equipped only with cameras struggle to perceive the full 3D environment around them, especially when objects are hidden behind other vehicles or buildings. This paper introduces a novel approach that allows multiple connected vehicles to share compact, lightweight 3D representations of their surroundings with each other, enabling each vehicle to "see" what others see and fill in its blind spots. Unlike previous methods that transmit large, hard-to-align data or require expensive depth sensors like LiDAR, this technique uses efficient 3D Gaussian primitives that naturally encode both shape and meaning, making sharing between vehicles straightforward and bandwidth-friendly. Experiments show that vehicles using this collaborative framework dramatically outperform single-vehicle perception, achieving gains of over 8 points in semantic accuracy while using as little as 35% of the communication bandwidth required by competing approaches. This work directly advances NSF's mission of improving transportation safety and efficiency by making cooperative autonomous driving more practical and accessible through affordable, camera-only hardware. The framework's ability to deliver high-quality 3D scene understanding with minimal communication overhead positions it as a foundational building block for scalable, real-world deployment of connected autonomous vehicles.
